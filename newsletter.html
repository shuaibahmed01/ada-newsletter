Legislation needed to delay Corporate Transparency Act filing deadline

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 10, 2024

The Corporate Transparency Act's year-end filing deadline for small businesses, including many dental practices, may be delayed by one year under H.R. 9278, introduced by Rep. Zachary Nunn. The ADA and 150 trade associations support this legislation, citing low compliance rates and lack of awareness. The act, aimed at combating money laundering, currently requires businesses with fewer than 20 employees or under $5 million in annual revenue to file beneficial ownership information by the end of 2024.

ADA comments on proposed Medicare payment policies for dental services in 2025

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 12, 2024

The ADA submitted comments to CMS on proposed Medicare payment policies for dental services in 2025, continuing its three-year engagement on this issue. The ADA supports coverage for dental services related to end-stage renal disease and dialysis, while requesting clarification on diabetes-related care. The association advocated for delayed implementation of certain coding requirements until 2026 and proposed standardizing care coordination processes. The ADA commended CMS for recognizing oral health's importance in overall well-being.

The ADA is advocating against broad interpretation of ERISA that could limit states' authority to regulate dental benefits. Dentists pay $1.7 million to settle false claims allegations

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 19, 2024

Two Connecticut dentists and their practices agreed to pay $1.7 million to settle allegations of violating federal and state False Claims Acts. From 2016 to 2018, they allegedly paid kickbacks to a patient recruiter for Medicaid referrals, violating their provider agreements and the Anti-Kickback Statute. The settlement reimburses the Medicaid program for the improper conduct.

Dentist pleads guilty to violating federal anti-kickback law

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 19, 2024

A Connecticut dentist pleaded guilty to violating federal anti-kickback laws between 2016 and 2023. She conspired with recruiters to bring Medicaid beneficiaries to appointments, paying over $360,000 in kickbacks. Connecticut Medicaid reimbursed her $2.2 million for these services. The dentist faces up to five years imprisonment and is currently released on bond pending sentencing.

ADA seeks representative for medical coding committee

Author: Mary Beth Versaci

Published: September 20, 2024

The American Dental Association is seeking nominations for an alternate representative on the American Medical Association's CPT Advisory Committee from September 21 to October 21, 2024. Building a legacy: Dr. Natalia Chalmers on her work at CMS

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 11, 2024

Advil Dual Action earns first ADA Seal for short-term pain relief

Author: Mary Beth Versaci

Published: September 23, 2024

The American Dental Association (ADA) awarded its first Seal of Acceptance for short-term pain relief to Advil Dual Action in September 2024. This new category supports ADA guidelines recommending NSAIDs for acute dental pain. The product, containing ibuprofen and acetaminophen, was recognized for its safety and efficacy. The ADA Seal program, established in 1931, helps dentists make informed recommendations about consumer products.
