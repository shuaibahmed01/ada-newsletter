ADA comments on proposed Medicare payment policies for dental services in 2025

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 12, 2024

The ADA submitted comments to CMS on proposed Medicare payment policies for dental services in 2025, marking the third consecutive year of such feedback. The ADA supports coverage for dental services related to end-stage renal disease and dialysis, while requesting clarification on diabetes-related care. They suggested delaying certain administrative requirements and ICD-10 implementation until 2026, and proposed adopting a standardized referral form for care coordination. The ADA commended CMS for recognizing oral health's importance in overall well-being.

DDH Compact Commission holds first meeting

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 09, 2024

The Dentist and Dental Hygienist Compact Commission held its first meeting on August 28, 2024, with representatives from 10 member states. The compact aims to allow dental professionals to practice across states without multiple licenses. Key discussions included potential rules, commission structure, and shared data systems. The next meeting is planned for early 2025, with applications for multi-state practice privileges expected in Fall 2025.

Legislation needed to delay Corporate Transparency Act filing deadline

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 10, 2024

The Corporate Transparency Act's filing deadline for small businesses, including many dental practices, may be delayed by one year under H.R. 9278, introduced by Rep. Zachary Nunn. The ADA and 150 trade associations support this legislation, citing low submission rates and lack of awareness among affected businesses. The act, aimed at combating money laundering, currently requires filing by year-end 2024. The proposed delay addresses concerns about the short reporting timeframe for small businesses.

Building a legacy: Dr. Natalia Chalmers on her work at CMS

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 11, 2024

Dr. Natalia Chalmers, a pediatric dentist and oral health policy expert, became the first chief dental officer at the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services in August 2021. With over 20 years of experience, she aims to improve oral health care and policy, focusing on health equity, access, and partnerships. Her role involves being the lead subject matter expert for CMS programs. Dr. Chalmers' work includes launching the Oral Health Cross-Cutting Initiative and modernizing Medicare's dental claims process.

Dentists pay $1.7 million to settle false claims allegations

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 19, 2024

Two Connecticut dentists and their practices agreed to pay $1.7 million to settle allegations of violating federal and state False Claims Acts between 2016-2018. They allegedly paid a patient recruiter $115 per Medicaid patient referred, violating the Anti-Kickback Statute and their provider agreements. This settlement reimburses the Medicaid program for the improper conduct.

Dentist pleads guilty to violating federal anti-kickback law

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 19, 2024

A Connecticut dentist pleaded guilty to violating the federal anti-kickback statute between 2016 and 2023. She conspired with recruiters to bring Medicaid beneficiaries to appointments, paying over $360,000 in kickbacks. Connecticut Medicaid reimbursed her $2.2 million for these services. The dentist faces up to five years imprisonment and is currently released on bond pending sentencing.
