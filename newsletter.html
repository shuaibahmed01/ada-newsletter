ADA comments on proposed Medicare payment policies for dental services in 2025

Olivia Anderson - September 12, 2024

The American Dental Association (ADA) submitted comments to the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) on proposed 2025 Medicare payment policies for dental services. The ADA supports coverage for patients with end-stage renal disease undergoing dialysis and requests clarification on various issues. They suggest delaying certain implementation requirements until 2026 and propose adopting the ADA Medicare Referral Form for care coordination.

House committee holds 50th ERISA anniversary hearing

Olivia Anderson - September 13, 2024

The House subcommittee on Health, Employment, Labor and Pensions held a hearing on September 10, 2024, to discuss ERISA's 50th anniversary and potential improvements. The ADA submitted comments emphasizing the importance of state authority in regulating dental insurance. The hearing focused on protecting employer-sponsored health benefits while balancing federal and state regulatory roles in healthcare.

Building a legacy: Dr. Natalia Chalmers on her work at CMS

Olivia Anderson - September 11, 2024

Dr. Natalia Chalmers, the first chief dental officer at CMS, is working to improve oral health care and policy. With over 20 years of experience, she focuses on health equity, access, and partnerships. Since joining CMS in 2021, she has launched initiatives to align programs, improve access, and modernize payment systems. Dr. Chalmers aims to address oral health disparities and promote prevention through data-driven strategies.

CODA seeks input on proposed standard revisions

Mary Beth Versaci - September 11, 2024

The Commission on Dental Accreditation (CODA) is seeking public input on proposed revisions to dental accreditation standards. A virtual hearing is scheduled for October 10, 2024, from 5-6 p.m. CDT. Interested parties can also submit comments online until December 1, 2024, for most standards, and until June 1, 2025, for orofacial pain education standards. The revisions affect various dental education programs.

Symposium to highlight orthodontic innovation, technology

Mary Beth Versaci - September 17, 2024

The International Orthodontics Foundation and ADA Forsyth Institute are hosting their first joint symposium on orthodontic innovation and technology on October 5-6, 2024. The event features 30 speakers from 24 institutions, including ADA Executive Director Raymond A. Cohlmia. It offers both in-person and virtual attendance options, with CE credits available for a fee or free registration for IOF members.
