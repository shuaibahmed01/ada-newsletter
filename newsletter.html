ADA seeks representative for medical coding committee

Author: Mary Beth Versaci

Published: September 20, 2024

The American Dental Association (ADA) is seeking nominations for an alternate representative on the American Medical Association's CPT Advisory Committee from September 21 to October 21, 2024. The committee advises on Current Procedural Terminology codes for various medical specialties. Nominees must have dual D.D.S./D.M.D. and M.D. degrees, AMA membership, and CPT process experience. The three-year position involves collaborating with the primary adviser and attending annual meetings.

Dentists pay $1.7 million to settle false claims allegations

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 19, 2024

Two Connecticut dentists and their practices agreed to pay $1.7 million to settle allegations of violating federal and state False Claims Acts between 2016 and 2018. They allegedly paid kickbacks to a patient recruiter for Medicaid referrals, violating their provider agreements and the Anti-Kickback Statute. The settlement reimburses the Medicaid program for the improper conduct.

Dentist pleads guilty to violating federal anti-kickback law

Author: Olivia Anderson

Published: September 19, 2024

A Connecticut dentist pleaded guilty to violating the federal anti-kickback statute between 2016 and 2023. She conspired with recruiters to bring Medicaid beneficiaries to appointments, paying over $360,000 in kickbacks. Connecticut Medicaid reimbursed her $2.2 million for these services. The dentist faces up to five years imprisonment and is currently released on bond pending sentencing.
